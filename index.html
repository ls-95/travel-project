Why Greenland?

The world's largest island

Greenland is a destination like no other, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, adventure, and cultural richness that makes it a must-visit for any traveler. The island's vast, pristine landscapes are dominated by towering glaciers, dramatic fjords, and the expansive Greenland Ice Sheet, providing breathtaking scenery and unparalleled photo opportunities. Visitors can witness the awe-inspiring Northern Lights during the winter months or experience the endless daylight of the Arctic summer. Whether it's hiking through rugged terrain, kayaking among icebergs, or simply enjoying the tranquility of this remote paradise, Greenland offers a sense of adventure and discovery that is hard to find elsewhere.

Beyond its natural wonders, Greenland is home to a vibrant Inuit culture with a history that spans thousands of years. Travelers can immerse themselves in the local traditions by visiting small, picturesque villages, where they can learn about traditional hunting and fishing practices, enjoy authentic Greenlandic cuisine, and experience the warmth and hospitality of the Inuit people. The island also boasts unique wildlife, including polar bears, whales, and seabirds, making it a haven for nature enthusiasts and wildlife photographers. With its combination of stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and opportunities for outdoor adventure, Greenland is a destination that promises an unforgettable and truly unique travel experience.

Polar Circle Marathon

The Polar Circle Marathon - often referred to as 'the coolest marathon on Earth' - takes place in Kangerlussuaq, Greenland. The endless ice and arctic tundra of this vast country are the backdrop for this unusual race, in which runners race through the soundless arctic desert past glacier tongues and moraine landscapes.

This is a rare opportunity to visit one of the most remote corners of the world while daring to take part in an extremely challenging running event.
